Our story

From a first meeting in a school hall to a busy group with its own home.

From a borrowed school hall to our own den. The milestones that built 115th Ballinteer. (Add photos or extra detail from your archives anytime.)

  • September 1975

    The group begins in Our Lady's school hall. The first regular meetings and the start of scouting in Ballinteer.

  • 1976

    115th Ballinteer is officially registered with Scouting Ireland at Larch Hill.

  • 1981

    Our own den is built, a permanent home for weekly programmes, camps prep, storage and group life.

  • 1983

    The final mortgage is paid off. The den belongs outright to the group and its members.

  • 2008

    Major refurbishment: external fire doors replaced, internal fire doors fitted, and walls replastered, bringing the building up to date for safety and comfort.

  • Since then

    Beavers, Cubs, Scouts, BVG (Ventures) and Rovers have kept the place busy: camps, badges, hikes and countless weeknight programmes. Today we also hire the den overnight to other Scout groups visiting Dublin and the mountains.

The den is for our youth members. It’s theirs to enjoy and make use of. We’re very proud to look after it and pass it on, generation after generation.

Got a memory, old photo or correction? We’d love to hear from past members and leaders. get in touch.
