Group info

Volunteer-led, youth-focused, and part of Scouting Ireland.

115th Ballinteer has 171 young people across 7 sections, supported by 36 volunteer leaders.

We offer programmes for young people from age 6 through to adults, from Beavers all the way through to Rovers. We’re part of Scouting Ireland, which sets training, safeguarding standards and the overall programme framework.

Scouting aims to encourage the development of young people so they achieve their full potential. Activities are chosen to cover six areas of personal growth, known as the SPICES: Social, Physical, Intellectual, Character, Emotional and Spiritual development.

We’re run entirely by volunteers: parents, former members and people from the community who give their time to plan safe, fun activities every week. Behind the scenes that means managing waiting lists, admin, vetting and training, writing 8-week programme plans, organising camps and hikes, and communicating with hundreds of families.

We trace our story from September 1975 in the school hall, through building the den in 1981, to today. See our history for the full timeline.

Can you help?

We’re always looking for volunteers, whether you want to become a section leader or just lend a hand. Skills we could particularly use right now:

  • Painters
  • Plumbers
  • Electricians
  • General DIY

Or if you’d like to get more involved with the programme, speak to any leader about becoming a section helper. Get in touch if you’d like to know more.
